In local anesthesia, the patient is awake during the surgery, only the intended part of the surgery is anesthetized. In general anesthesia, the patient is in a state of anesthesia for the entire body, similar to deep sleep, with the help of a device to regulate breathing “mechanical ventilation”.

In Regional anesthesia for bariatric surgery, the entire abdominal area is anesthetized, including the upper abdomen (the intended area for obesity surgery). As for spinal anesthesia, it can be used for surgeries of the lower part of the abdominal area only, such as caesarean section and inguinal hernia repair.

The gastric sleeve operation acts by reducing the size of the stomach, thus eating much less quantities than before and so, fewer calories are gained, in addition to reducing the hunger hormone in order to reduce appetite and the feeling of hunger.

While the gastric bypass surgery, reducing the absorption of food and drink consumed inside the intestine is added to reducing the size of stomach as a result of diverting food from the calculated portion of the intestine.

The two operations share the need of the patient to adhere to a healthy lifestyle after recovery to maintain the desired result mainly by reducing the calories gained and exercising. But the need to take vitamins is at least a year after the gastric sleeve and continuously after gastric bypass.

It is usually preferable to avoid performing a gastric sleeve operation for those who suffer from stomach-related diseases, such as: esophageal reflux or hiatal hernia.

Diabetics or people who are sweet eaters benefit from gastric bypass surgery better. Also it is considered the best choice for correcting previous bariatric surgeries.

There are many types of gastric bypass, and each of them has a function and few types have been approved by the International Federation for the Surgery of Obesity and Metabolic Disorders “IFSO” due to the presence of sustained and positive results of these surgeries on the short and long terms, with negligible side effects and they include the classic gastric bypass and the mini gastric bypass

There are other types that are still in the process of experimenting, and their results are still not completely clear, such as: SADI and SASI.
